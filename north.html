North By Northwest


North by Northwest is part espionage, part romance and part thriller, but moulded in full Hitchcockian fashion. Alfred Hitchcock has managed to combine all these elements into a complete film filled with deceit and red herring. Here’s the short synopsis, The film about is about an advertising agent Roger Thornhill (played by Cary Grant) Who got caught between the crossfire of the CIA and Vandmamm, a spy and wrongly accused of killing a UN Diplomatic. And How all things panned out, it’s told in a pure Hitchcock fashion.

Bomb under the table theory:

Hitchcock proposed an idea called ”Bomb under the table". It’s the simple distinction between suspense and surprise, which goes on like this, for example, take a scenario where you in a restaurant and unbeknownst to you there’s a bomb under the table that you're sitting which explodes—hold on to this scenario we’ll be comeback to this moment later. Consider the same scenario where you’re sitting at the table, but now you know there’s a bomb ready to go off at any minute – what will you do to stop it?? Is there any way you can escape??. Did you get any clue where this whole passage leading up to if you get its great if not it's fine because MB is going to take care of everything. Those scenarios are combinedly called “Bomb under the Table". According to Hitchcock if the bomb goes off, it'll create surprise and shock which will only last for a few moments. But what if you know there's a bomb under the desk and there's a timer, this creates suspense which makes viewers latch on to their seat belts. Now it’s not the fact the bomb explodes, but how the bomb it's going off. The moments of impending doom and tension that this thing may or may not go off from the table is what makes Alfred Hitchcock -The master of suspense. Most of the modern films totally discard the value of building suspense by opting the easy out. Now I'm not saying that the "shock" factor is inherently a bad thing, it's just that there are plethora of ways to create good cinema instead of cheap jumpscares and mindless shooting(that's coming from the guy who loves MI and John Wick franchises). It's maybe the time for filmmakers to take the audience seriously and respect their IQs. Now we’ll see how Hitchcock achieves those things in the movie North by Northwest.
